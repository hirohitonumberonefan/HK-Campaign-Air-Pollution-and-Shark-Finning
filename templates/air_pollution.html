Only 10% of the world population breathes in clean air.



Below, shows the trend of the Ozone levels in Hong Kong from the years 2000 - 2019.



As seen from the charts above, air pollution is a serious problem nowadays. Air pollution is caused by particles, both solid and liquid, suspended in the air. They mostly come from cars, trucks, factories, and wildfires.

These tiny particles in the air, are very very deadly. They can give you symptoms such as asthma, lung cancer, Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases, and autism.

In places where air pollution is very very serious (e.g New Delhi), even with the highest quality air filters, people can lose about a entire year of their life.





Air pollution started becoming a problem way back in the 1760s, when the Industrial Revolution started. Steam power became much more common, and levels of Carbon Dioxide rocketed, causing smog (smoky fog) on the streets. The air became very polluted, and killed many people.





In places like Hong Kong, air pollution might not be the worst, but still enough to cause drastic effects on health to people who are sensitive to certain pollutants. Hong Kong has a pollution index of 67.31, which is considered acceptable. However, it can get worse, and it WILL get worse, if we do not stop (the chart above proves that the ozone levels are getting higher over the years, which means air pollution in Hong Kong is getting worse).
