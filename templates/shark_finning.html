Shark Finning

100000000

Yep. That's the amount of sharks killed every year.

Shark Finning is a cruel way to extract the fins from sharks. The sharks are cut off from their fins and dumped back into the ocean, where they can't swim or breathe without their fins, and suffocate to death.





The history of shark finning dates back to the Ming dynasty. During those times, it was made into soup and only eaten by emperors. At the time, it was extremely hard to find and kill a shark. It was prized not for its flavor, but for its soft, jelly-like texture. Back then, shark fins would be added to some broth, and flavored with some form of meat. Shark fin soup quickly rose up the ranks as a symbol of power in China.





Today, shark fin soup has only grown in popularity. As the wealth of middle class people in China grew, so did the demand for shark fin soup. Other countries have also invested in shark fins, making it a worldwide product. Over 100 million sharks are killed every year to fulfill the demand of shark fins. Hong Kong has played a big role on this, having over 50% of all shark fins imported there.
